Very nice, how much?

Now that you've defined precisely what you want, it's time to figure out how much you need. For this, you need to know advanced calculus.

Just kidding, you just need to know addition. 1

We'll walk through this example by me figuring out how much I need to accomplish my goals.

Here they are from the last post:

  1. Move out of my parent's place and into an apartment I can call my own and prepay six months of rent.
  2. Take three months off work to work out.

Starting with #1: How much do I need to prepay for six month's worth of rent in San Francisco?

Well, let's figure that out.

Ok, well, if I search for the average rent in San Francisco, the first result that comes up is from Curbed.com. They say this:

Time to go be a landlord, shit

That's what they have found to be the new normal price for a one-bedroom apartment in the city.

What the fuck? Who in their right mind pays over $40k per year for a bed and a place to shit? If you're going to live and work in San Francisco where the average shitty tech job keeps you at work 60+ hours a week and hungover from the Friday happy hour, that leaves quite a small amount of time for your to enjoy the place that's eating up over 40% of your salary.

Anyway, that's not going to work for me. Let's keep looking.

The link that goes to Rentcafe has a table that lists the average rents by apartment type:

That's better. Still time to go be a landlord

That's headed in the right direction. The average studio is $1k less than the average one bedroom. They're basically the same thing though, so I'm a bit confused how one is priced so much less. 2

Another thing is that I'll be sharing the room with my girlfriend, so the rent will be cut in half.

Ok, well I still think it's a bit high, but for now I'd say I'm comfortable setting my rent ceiling at $1200.

Six times that is $7200.

Adding in the security deposit, which in San Francisco can be as much as twice the rent, we have $9600. I'll round this up for good measure.

Goal one target amount: $10000

On to #2. I need to fund my living expenses for three months. So the amount for the second goal is simply three times my monthly expenses.

Well, I know what that is today. But, since I'm doing this from scratch, I'll be starting a new budget. There are also things that I'll have to ballpark that I don't have right now, like a gym membership and maybe a personal trainer. After a few cycles, I'll be able to determine this number, so we'll revisit the target amount for this goal later.

See you in the next step!
