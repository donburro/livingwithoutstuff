I've been meaning to get this up here for a while. National Preparedness Month has come and gone and I have a month's worth of transactions to categorize.

I started fresh on a new budget at the beginning of the month, adding only notes to the items to jog my memory for proper categorization. I want to keep my budget as simple as possible, so I'll be categorizing transactions into only three groups,

  1. capital expenses
  2. necessary operational expenses, and
  3. discretionary operation expenses,

which, together, will capture any type of expense. Whether it be a one-time furniture purchase, a once-in a while taxi ride, or the monthly rent expense, it'll fit into one of those categories. Notes will assist me if I want to do further investigation and analysis later down the line.

I will keep another category for things that I want to allocate money towards, like monthly investment amounts or buying a course. I will use the Investments category for this for now.

I will not track cashback or expenses incurred by me on behalf of my employer.

Definitions

Before we begin, let's define the categories a bit further to help in categorization.

  • CapEx
    One time or infrequent expenses.
  • Necessary OpEx
    Repeating expenses that are absolutely necessary to my survival maintaining a decent standard of living. On top of things that are necessary to survival (food, water) are things that help me maintain a certain standard of living (shelter, personal hygiene products).
  • Discretionary OpEx
    What does not fall into necessary OpEx category. Repeating expenses that can be avoided if money became an issue. Things I could live without if I lost all ability to make money and had to really turn the dial down on my expenses.

The purpose of breaking up OpEx into two categories is to determine the minimum amount I should keep in my emergency fund: Twelve months of Necessary OpEx.
